1 Princes Parade

Boathouse, Seabrook (c.1915)

Real photographic postcard showing the old boathouse viewed from the beach along Princes Parade. The Battery Wall and Hospital Hill rise in the background, capturing Seabrook’s early coastal landscape. Held in the on-site archives.

Beach, Seabrook Looking Towards Sandgate (c.1960s)

Real photographic postcard by Firth, showing a busy seaside scene along the promenade with beachgoers, fishing boats, and the coastline stretching towards Sandgate. Captures the mid-20th century character of Seabrook as a popular coastal destination. Held in the on-site archives.

260 Seabrook Road

Eastcott Terrace, Seabrook (c. mid-20th century)

Reproduction photograph showing Eastcott Terrace along Seabrook Road, including part of Seabrook School. Published as H.B. F4L 702, the image captures the character of this residential stretch in its earlier form. Held in the on-site archives.

188 Seabrook Road

Seabrook Looking East (c. early 20th century)

Valentine Series postcard showing Seabrook Road viewed eastwards, opposite what is now 188 Seabrook Road. The image captures a quieter streetscape of terraced housing and everyday life in the village. Held in the on-site archives.
