Seabrook Village Kent

Welcome to Seabrook Village Kent

Seabrook is a small coastal village in Kent, set between Hythe and Sandgate, with the sea at the bottom and the Kent Downs rising behind it. It offers a mix of seaside calm and countryside views, all within roughly an hour of London.

The village is named after the brook that runs alongside Horn Street, flowing into the Royal Military Canal before reaching the sea. This historic waterway adds to Seabrook’s character and provides a peaceful route for walking and wildlife.

Places of Interest

The Fountain

A Village Landmark

Nestled on Seabrook Road at the heart of our village, The Fountain (also known as the Fountain Hotel) is one of Seabrook’s most beloved landmarks. The building dates from 1888, standing on the site of an earlier inn, and today it still exudes charm with its light, airy bar, traditional décor, and welcoming atmosphere.

Inside, you’ll find a warm and friendly pub where locals and visitors alike gather to enjoy real ales, good company, and hearty, old-fashioned pub grub using locally sourced ingredients.
